OcuClaw · the OpenClaw client for Even Realities' Even Hub & G2

Agentic dev for the G2 — across a whole fleet, not one busy laptop.

OcuClaw is the OpenClaw client that runs AI agents on Even Realities' Even Hub and the G2 smart glasses. Building for it means looping the OcuClaw app (Kotlin/Compose) plus an OpenClaw plugin through a G2 simulator and real hardware. Fleet Control Center turns that single-machine grind into a fleet of ready boxes you drive from one screen — or your phone. Run many build-and-test cycles at once, watch any box's live glasses or phone UI, and move branches between sim, phone, and G2 lanes.

Bottleneck → gone
One sim, one machine
No more waiting your turn for the local simulator — the fleet runs many end-to-end loops in parallel.
Mix-up → gone
One client, many versions
A local OpenClaw client can't cleanly host two plugin versions at once. Each box is its own isolated stack.
Friction → gone
Branches on sim or G2
Arm a box as a simulator cockpit or a live phone/glasses client; serve static builds publicly when a browser bundle is enough.

Plain commands and natural language on top. Underneath, it handles the per-box relays, private tunnels, renewable leases, the one-client rule, and the single tailnet door — so you never have to think about any of it.

01 System map

One backend. Three ways in. Everything local but one door.

The browser talks only to a PC-local backend (fleet-cc, 127.0.0.1:7317), which reaches each box over private tunnels. Three ways to use a live box: overview (parked boxes stay cold; armed ones show a live glasses thumbnail), cockpit (drive one live — glasses + app panes, diagnostics tabs), and phone (your phone becomes the box's client). Temporary *.sprites.app URLs are a separate static-build lane; the live relay stays private.

The one rule that makes it all work Each box answers to exactly one client at a time — the cockpit's sim or the glasses, never both. Two clients on one box would fight over the same agent. Arming enforces it; diagnostics don't count.
02 The hands-off AI loop

Hand a box a task. It writes, builds, tests, reviews, and pushes the branch.

This is where the fleet earns its keep. Dispatch a self-contained task and the box runs the whole cycle on its own, detached — it survives your disconnect. Fire several at once across the fleet and walk away: different branches, no shared state, no waiting. You come back to finished branches and DONE mail. Codex and Claude are both headless dispatch engines, each cross-reviewed by the other one on the box, so every DONE carries a second opinion and a review.txt before you fetch and open the PR.

03 Disposable by design

Spin up as many boxes as you want. Near-zero cost while they sleep.

Built on

The fleet rides on Sprites, Fly's stateful-sandbox platform. Each box is a hardware-isolated Firecracker microVM with a persistent filesystem — a real Linux computer that's still there, exactly as you left it, the moment it wakes. Live copy-on-write checkpoints land in around 300 ms, a parked box restores and reconnects in well under a second, and an idle box hibernates at near-zero cost. Sprites is purpose-built to run AI agents — which is exactly what each box does — so you keep as many as you like, parked cold, and only pay for the seconds one is actually working.

Reproduce a user's bug on their own device The same disposability powers remote debugging. Serve an instrumented debug bundle on the fly behind an unguessable URL, hand it to a beta tester or bug reporter who isn't on your tailnet, and watch their live boot/trace beacon as they hit the issue on their own device — then tear the box down. No access to their machine required.
04 Inside one box

Every box is a complete, isolated OcuClaw stack.

A Sprites box (8 GB) running its own OpenClaw gateway, relay, G2 simulator, and a dev server for the OcuClaw app itself. Only that app server is web-fronted (the public *.sprites.app URL); the rest is loopback-only and reached over private tunnels. The G2 sim runs headless under a virtual display — the same simulator as the host, no GPU needed.

05 Phone lane

Your phone becomes the box — glasses UI, touch, and voice.

A phone arm stops the box's sim and hands the phone the role of the single client. The PC serves the box at a private per-box address; Fleet CC shows a QR or direct Even Hub link over the tailnet. Real input, real voice, real agent — just not on the public internet.

That's one of two ways onto your phone. The other: install the control center itself as a PWA and drive the whole fleet from your phone's browser — overview, cockpit, fullscreen glasses, milestone pushes, and per-box galleries.

06 One terminal, plain English

Run the whole fleet by asking — the ocuclaw-fleet skill.

Behind all of this is a stack of precise commands. The ocuclaw-fleet skill puts a natural-language front on them: you describe the work — a smoke test, a feature with full E2E coverage, a branch on real G2 — and it picks the right verbs, runs them, and reports back. One terminal does it all — and the same thing works from your phone, since the always-on PC answers natural language too. Reversible asks just run; anything that costs money or destroys state stops to confirm first.

Under the hood it's an intent router: your sentence maps to the right verbs — status, dispatch, sim-arm, login-status, setup-codex, park — run against spritectl, fleet-cc, and mail, then summarized back to you.

07 The bake cycle

Know what every box is running — and keep it recoverable.

Each box is baked from a branch and saved as a checkpoint — that checkpoint is what parks and wakes in under a second. Fleet CC now shows branch, commit, auth, gateway, and telemetry health, so freshness is visible before you spend a long rebuild.

Bake
Branch → checkpoint
A box is provisioned from a branch and snapshotted. That checkpoint is the thing that comes back online in under a second, every time.
Discover
Pets first, lab boxes visible
Primary boxes stay the main roster; scratch and test ocuclaw-* boxes show up as limited lab entries when they exist.
Rebake / retrofit
Full refresh or fast tooling repair
Rebake means rebuild, reprovision, gateway restart, and checkpoint. setup-codex repairs Codex/OpenClaw tooling without a full rebuild.
Security in one line Fleet CC is loopback-first, tailnet-host gated when exposed, and mutating routes are origin-checked. Public *.sprites.app URLs are temporary static-build doors; widening hosts or auth still asks first.

Less waiting. Less friction. One sentence drives the fleet.
